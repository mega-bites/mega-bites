In the UK, we throw away £13 billion of food each year - costing the average family around £60 a month. Few of us set out to waste so much food on purpose, but a lack of forward planning, not storing food so it lasts and confusion over the meaning of date labels, can quickly add up to lots of food being thrown away.
  • 40% of the 16 million tonnes of food we throw away annually ends up in landfill.
  • 20 million tonnes of greenhouse gas emissions are linked with UK food waste - around 85% of this is associated with food production, and 15% with its disposal.
  • 19000 square kilometres (an area more than nine tenths the size of Wales) would be needed to produce all the food subsequently discarded by UK households.
